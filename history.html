    01.HISTORY ?

The history of the website can be traced back to 1989 when British scientist Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web (WWW):

1989: Berners-Lee first proposed the WWW while working at CERN, the European Organization for Nuclear Research.
1990: The first website, server, and browser went live at CERN.
1991: The first web page was published on August 6, 1991, by Berners-Lee. The page was dedicated to the WWW project and was hosted on Berners-Lee's NeXT computer.
1993: The WWW was put into the public domain by CERN. The World Wide Web began to be used in everyday life.
1994: The first International World Wide Web conference was held at CERN.
1995: The number of websites had grown to around 23,500.
The early web pages were mostly text-based, and lacked special design or page structure. However, they did include basic HTML tags for headers, paragraphs, and links. The history of the web has seen many changes, including the ability to shop online, work from home, and connect with people around the world.
